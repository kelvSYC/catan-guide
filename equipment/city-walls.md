**City Walls** are an add-on to cities introduced in [_Cities & Knights_][cities-knights] that allows its owner to raise their hand limit. Subsequent to their introduction, they were also made part of [_Atlantis_][atlantis].

## Appearance
City walls were originally introduced as flat square pieces, for which the city piece would rest upon. In the 1st international edition, a sticker can also be applied to the piece for decorative purposes to give the piece a brick wall pattern.

Since the 2nd international edition, city walls are ring pieces that are attached to the city figure. Viking wood pieces are also ring-shaped pieces, meant to slot the city figure within.

English 6th edition pieces are larger square pieces, but also have a groove used to rest the city piece.

In [_Atlantis_][atlantis], city walls are cardboard tokens that a player can rest their city pieces on top of. Unlike their [_Cities & Knights_][cities-knights] counterparts, these are not player pieces.

## Gameplay
A city may be upgraded to have city walls. Each city wall on the board raises the hand limit of its owner by two cards.

- If at any point a city with city walls is reduced to a settlement, the city walls are removed. This lowers the player's hand limit.

## Manifest
3 city walls are included for the red, blue, orange, and white players in [_Cities & Knights_][cities-knights], and 3 city walls are included for the green and purple (brown before the English 6th edition / 4th international edition) in the [_Cities & Knights_][cities-knights] extension. Note that a Viking wood [_Cities & Knights_][cities-knights] set includes 4 city wall pieces, but only 3 are used in a given game.

7 neutral city walls are included in [_Atlantis_][atlantis].
